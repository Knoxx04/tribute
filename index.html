David Goggins

The man. The legend. The myth

Goggins was born on February 17, 1975, to Trunnis and Jackie Goggins.

Worked as a TACP from 1994 until 1999 alongside British counterparts Flt Sgt Jones, Flt Stg Nair and Pvt Noble.

He graduated from BUD/S training with BUD/S class 235 in 2001.

In 2004, Goggins graduated from Army Ranger School, and received the "Enlisted Honor Man" award, receiving a 100% peer evaluation.

In 2005, Goggins entered the San Diego One Day, a 24-hour ultramarathon in San Diego.

Goggins was invited to the 2006 Badwater-135, where he finished 5th overall.

In 2007, Goggins placed third overall in the Badwater-135.He competed in the Badwater-135 in 2013 and finished 18th, after a break from the event since 2008.[25]

In 2008, he was named a "Hero of Running" by Runner's World.In 2016, Goggins won the Infinitus 88k in 12 hours. In the same year, he won the Music City Ultra 50k, and Strolling Jim 40 Miler.

In 2020, Goggins ran the Moab 240 ultramarathon, placing 2nd in the 241-mile event with a time of 63 hours and 21 minutes.

To date David Goggins is one of the most influential male figures in the society.
